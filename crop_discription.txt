1. Pepper
A perennial climbing vine valued for its spice. It is typically grown on "standards" or support trees like Silver Oak or Erythrina.

Growing Conditions: Thrives in humid, tropical climates with high annual rainfall and partial shade. Requires soil rich in organic matter.

Harvest: Typically occurs between January and March.

2. Coffee 
A major plantation crop primarily comprising Robusta and Arabica varieties.

Growing Conditions: Requires an elevation between 700m–1200m. "Blossom Showers" (early spring rains) are critical for flowering and yield. Often serves as a shade provider for intercropped species.

Harvest: Robusta (January–March); Arabica (November–January).

3. Banana 
A fast-growing herbaceous plant, with varieties like Nendran and Red Banana being common.

Growing Conditions: Requires deep, well-drained loamy soil and significant water intake. It is highly sensitive to waterlogging and high wind speeds, which can damage the heavy bunches.

Harvest: Generally available year-round.

4. Ginger 
A herbaceous perennial grown for its edible rhizome.

Growing Conditions: Prefers well-drained sandy or lateritic loam. It is an "exhaustive" crop that depletes soil nutrients quickly, requiring regular crop rotation. Sensitive to rhizome rot in poorly drained areas.

Harvest: Typically December to February.

5. Tapioca 
A hardy tuberous root crop often used as a staple food source.

Growing Conditions: Extremely resilient and drought-tolerant. It thrives in laterite soils and can produce yields in lower-quality soil where other crops might fail.

Harvest: Generally ready 6 to 10 months after planting.
